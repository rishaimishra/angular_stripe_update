Background
A. The Tutor is the owner of the Intellectual Property including certain goodwill relating to the Materials and the Licensed Products;
B. SL wishes to obtain the exclusive right (together with the Tutor) to promote, market and sell the Materials and Licensed Products via the SL Platform on the term set out in this Agreement;
C. The Tutor will be responsible for the creation and ongoing development of the Materials and the Licensed Products.

1. INTERPRETATION

  Agreement means this Agreement, its Schedules and any Annexure.
   
  Claim means each claim, suit, action, demand, right, proceeding, litigation, investigation, notice, judgment, cost, loss, damage, expense or liability, wherever and however arising, whether present, future, ascertained, unascertained, actual, prospective, contingent, direct or indirect.
  Commencement Date means the date of this Agreement.
   
  Confidential Information means (whether or not in material form and whether or not disclosed before or after the date of this Agreement) any information of whatever kind disclosed, revealed, learnt, accessible or made available by one party (the Disclosing Party) to the other party (the Receiving Party) under, in connection with or in relation to this Agreement that:
   
 
(a) is by its nature confidential;
(b) is designated by the Disclosing Party as confidential;
(c) is regulated under relevant legislation (including payment and personal information); or
(d) the Receiving Party knows or reasonably ought to know is confidential,

2. DURATION
   
  This Agreement commences on the Commencement Date and will continue indefinitely subject to the right of either party to terminate on 30 days written notice.
   
3. LICENCE
   
 
(a) Subject only to clause 3(b), the Tutor grants to SL the exclusive, worldwide, perpetual, transferable license (together with the right to sublicense) to promote market and make Online Sales of the Materials and Licensed Products.
(b) Without derogating from clause 3(a), the Tutor may himself (or through any corporate entity of which he is the primary shareholder or beneficiary) exploit the Intellectual Property in any manner he deems fit, including via Online Sales.
   
4. ONGOING DEVELOPMENT
   
 
(a) The Tutor may in his discretion and at any time further develop the Materials.
(b) SL is under no obligation to support or enhance the Materials.

5. Tutor’S RESPONSIBILITIES
   
5.1 Responsibilities
   
  For the duration of this Agreement, Tutor must promote and market the Licensed Products and the SL Platform to its client base and generally provide to SL such assistance and support as may be required in relation to the marketing and sale of the Materials and Licensed Products;
   
5.2 Recording Rights
   
 
(a) Each of Tutor and SL may video or audio tape (Record) each or any Event for future instructional and/or commercial use and shall retain a non-exclusive right to promote, distribute, stream and sell any such audio or video recording.
(b) If either Tutor and SL chooses to Record an Event, then each of Tutor and SL, as the case may be, agrees to bear and pay the cost of all equipment, sound and lighting, etc. for such purposes.
   
6. FEES
   
 
(a) In consideration of the rights granted to SL under clause 3, SL must pay to Tutor the Fees set out in Schedule1.
(b) Fees must be paid on a monthly basis to the Tutor’s online wallet and may be paid (up to 50%) in Success Coins.
(c) Fee swill be paid in accordance with a sales report to be created by SL and delivered to Tutor by the 7th day of each month in respect of all Online Sales made during the previous month.
(d) The Fees are quoted exclusive of any applicable value added taxes, goods and services tax and any other consumption tax.
(e) If any tax is payable to any authority as a consequence of any supply made (or deemed to be made) by Tutor to SL in connection with this Agreement, then the tax payable in respect of the supply must be paid by SL.
(f) If any withholding tax is levied on any Fee then SL must pay the amount of that withholding tax directly to the relevant authority and the Fee payable et Tutor will be reduced by the amount so paid.
   
7. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY
   
 
(a) The Tutor retains ownership in and all right, title and interest to the Intellectual Property and the Material sand Licensed Products.
(b) SL retains ownership in and all right, title and interest to the database of persons visiting the SL Platform, streaming an Event or purchasing the Materials or Licensed Products Tutor may access this database free of any consideration to provide marketing materials to any person who will have opted in to receive marketing materials from Tutor.
(c) Tutor warrants and represents to SL that he (or an entity owned by him) has created and designed the Materials and Licensed Products and that in so doing he has not infringed the proprietary rights of any other person.
(d) Tutor hereby indemnifies and holds SL harmless against any and all losses, liabilities, Claims, costs and expenses, including legal costs and expenses incurred by SL arising out of, or in connection with any claim, infringement, or alleged infringement or use of the Intellectual Property in the Materials or Licensed Products.

8. CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION
   
8.1 Restrictions on use and disclosure
   
 
(a) The Receiving Party may only use the Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party for the purposes of performing its obligations and exercising its rights under this Agreement.
(b) Subject to clause 8.2, the Receiving Party must keep the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information confidential and must not (except as expressly permitted under this Agreement):
 
(i) disclose or use the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information; or
(ii) make copies of material containing the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.
(c) The Receiving Party must take all possible steps to safeguard the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information and comply with any requirements specified by the Disclosing Party from time to time.
(d) The Receiving Party must implement security practices against any unauthorised copying, use, disclosure, access and damage or destruction of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.
(e) (e)The Receiving must notify the Disclosing Party if it:
 
(i) suspects or becomes aware of any unauthorised access, copying, use, disclosure, damage destruction of the other party’s Confidential Information, in any form; or
(ii) is required by law to disclose any of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.
(f) Each party must take all reasonable steps to enforce any obligation of confidentiality imposed or required to be imposed by this Agreement.
(g) Each party must do all things, execute all documents and give all reasonably required assistance to the other party to enforce any obligation of confidence imposed required to be imposed by this Agreement.
   
   
8.2 Permitted disclosures
   
  The Receiving Party may disclose the Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party:
   
 
(a) to those of its personnel who need to know such Confidential Information and have agreed with the Receiving Party to keep such Confidential Information confidential on terms substantially similar in all material respects to this clause;
(b) to the extent required by law or in order to comply with any rules or regulations of any internationally recognised securities exchanges; or
(c) with the prior written consent of the Disclosing Party.
   
8.3 Right to injunctive relief
   
  The Receiving Party acknowledges that monetary damages may not be a sufficient remedy for a breach of its obligations under this clause and that the Disclosing Party will be entitled, without waiving any of its other rights or remedies, to such injunctive or equitable relief as may be deemed proper by a court of competent jurisdiction if any such breach occurs.

8.4 Period of confidentiality
   
  The obligations with respect to Confidential Information disclosed under this Agreement will survive termination or expiry of this Agreement and continue for as long as the information remains confidential.
   
9. TERMINATION
   
 
(a) This Agreement will endure indefinitely subject to the right of either party to terminate on giving to the other 30 days’ notice in writing.
(b) A party may immediately (or with effect from any later date it may nominate) terminate this Agreement by written notice to the other party if:
 
(i) the other party materially breaches this Agreement, such breach is capable of remedy and the defaulting party fails to remedy such breach within twenty (20) Business Days of receipt of notice from the aggrieved party specifying the breach;
(ii) a receiver, receiver and manager, an official manager, a controller, a liquidator, a provisional liquidator, an administrator or other like person is appointed for the whole or substantially the whole of the other party’s assets, undertaking or business; or
(iii) if the other party becomes insolvent or is otherwise unable to pay its debts as and when they become due.
(c) The expiry or termination of this Agreement will not affect or limit any accrued rights of the parties.
   
10. AMENDMENTS
   
  Any variation of this Agreement (save for changes to the Fees) including its Schedules must be in writing and signed by the parties.
   
   
11. WAIVER
   
 
(a) A waiver by a party of a provision of or a right under this Agreement or of any right of election arising from a breach of this Agreement must be in writing and signed by an authorised officer of party granting the waiver.
(b) A waiver is effective only in the specific instance and for the specific purpose for which it is given.
(c) A breach of this Agreement or any right of election arising from a breach of this Agreement is not waived by any failure or delay in the exercise, or partial exercise, of that or any other right.
   
   
12. SEVERABILITY
   
 
(a) The covenants, undertakings and conditions (and each and every part of them) contained in this Agreement are severable and will be construed so as to not infringe any laws of the Jurisdiction or any other relevant jurisdiction.
(b) If any covenant, undertaking or condition, on its true interpretation, is found to infringe any such laws, that covenant, undertaking or condition will be read down to such extent as may be necessary to ensure that it does not infringe any such laws and, as may be reasonable in all the circumstances, to give it a valid operation of a partial character.
(c) If the infringing covenant, undertaking or condition cannot be so read down, it will be deemed void and severable and will be deemed to be deleted from this Agreement as if it was never incorporated in this Agreement and the parties will negotiate in good faith for the purpose of substituting an appropriate clause of this Agreement, so far as is practicable, in lieu of the deleted covenant, undertaking or condition.

13. RELATIONSHIP
   
 
(a) The relationship between the parties is one of licensee and Tutor. No party has the authority to bind the other party by contract or otherwise and no party, nor their employees or agents, are employees or agents of another party.
(b) Each party assumes full responsibility for the actions of its personnel while they are performing services pursuant to this Agreement and shall be solely responsible for their supervision, direction and control, payment of salary including any taxes and superannuation, workers compensation, disability benefits and the like.
   
14. FORCE MAJEURE
   
 
(a) A party will not be liable for any failure or delay in the performance of its obligations under this Agreement if that failure or delay is due to circumstances beyond that party’s control (Force Majeure). Any party who is, by reason of Force Majeure, unable to perform any obligation or condition under this Agreement must notify the other parties as soon as possible specifying:
 
(i) the cause and extent of such non-performance;
(ii) the date of commencement of non-performance; and
(iii) the means proposed to be adopted to remedy or abate the Force Majeure.
(b) A party who is, by reason of Force Majeure, unable to perform any obligation or condition under this Agreement must:
 
(i) use all commercially reasonable endeavours to remedy or abate the Force Majeure as quickly as possible;
(ii) resume performance as quickly as possible after cessation of the Force Majeure; and
(iii) notify each party when the Force Majeure has terminated or abated to an extent which permits resumption of performance to occur.
(c) Subject to any other termination provisions, a non-performance by either of the parties of any obligation or condition under this Agreement shall be excused during the time and to the extent that such performance is prevented, wholly or in part, by an event of Force Majeure of which notice has been given under clause 0.
   
15. ENTIRE AGREEMENT
   
  This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement of the parties about its subject matter and supersedes any previous understandings or agreements on that subject matter. Each party acknowledges that in entering into this Agreement, that it:
   
 
(a) has not relied on any statement made or conduct engaged in by another party or any person on behalf of another party other than the statements set out in this Agreement;
(b) was not influenced or induced to enter into this Agreement by any statement or conduct of the type referred to in clause 16(a).
