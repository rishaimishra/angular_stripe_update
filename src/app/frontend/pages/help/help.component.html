Crypto Dictionary

Atomic Swap- is a smart contract technology that enables exchange of one cryptocurrency for another without using centralized go betweens, such as exchanges.

Airdrop- The simplest way to define Airdrop is free tokens. Typically, to qualify for the free tokens, you are required to perform a set of instructions. E.g. Referrals, liking a social page, providing email address etc.

Altcoin- It is an alternative Cryptocurrecy created after the success of bitcoin. But aside from bitcoin, there are hundreds of other digital currencies out there. These are known as “altcoins,” or alternatives to bitcoin; for example, ether, ripple, zcash, monero and dash, to name just a few.

AML- It is an abbreviation of Anti Money Laundering. It refers to a set of law, regulations and procedures to deter the practice of generating income through illegal activities

ASIC Chip- An abbreviation of Application-specific integrated circuit. It is a chip used to mine Bitcoin due to its cost and efficiency

Bitcoin- It is a digital and global money system currency. It allow Peer to Peer transfer of money across the internet. Also, the first cryptocurrency created.

Block- Transaction data is stored in file called “Block”. They can be thought of as the individual pages of a city recorder's recordbook (where changes to title to real estate are recorded) or a stock transaction ledger. Blocks are organized into a linear sequence over time (also known as the block chain).

Blockchain- is a distributed data existing on multiple computer at the same time.

Block Reward- refers to the new Bitcoin that are rewarded by Crypto network to each miner for each block they mine successfully

Consensus- It is achieved when all participants of the network agree on the validity of the transactions

Cryptocurrency- Digital assets also known as tokens

Coinmarketcap.com- It is a website for tracing capitalization of various cryptocurrency & Crypto exchange

Cryptographic Hash Function- a hash functions take an input and returns a fixed size in alphanumeric string. The application of the hash function is to message authentication, digital signatures. It is also a security requirement for cryptographic hash functions, security against brute-force attacks and cryptanalysis

CEX- An abbreviation of Centralized exchange where the operating company acts as the middle man in order to fulfil the trades on the platform.

Cold Storage- A hardware wallet to store cryptocurrency. For instances, Trezor and Nano Ledger

Candle Stick- A type of price chart that displays the high, low, open and closing prices of a token for a specific period

DApp- Decentralized application has its backend code that runs on a decentralized Peer to Peer network

Distributed Ledger- A database held and updated by participants in a large network

DEX- Decentralized exchange does not rely on a centralized entity to hold the customer’s tokens. Instead, it works in a Peer to Peer fashion where a trade occurs through an automatic process.

Due Diligence- refers to prudence, care and extensive investigation prior to a decision.

Desktop wallet- software wallets that are downloaded and installed on your desktop. For instances, Exodus wallet, Jaxx etc

Encryption- encoding data, especially to restrict unauthorized access. Form of security

Ethereum- a software platform based on blockchain technology where it allows developers to build and deploy on decentralized application

Ether- The company Ethereum creates a token known as “Ether” ETH

Erc-20- a standard protocol that defines certain rules and standards for issuing tokens on its application. ERC stands for Ethereum request for comments and 20 is the number assigned to the request

Ethgasstation- full transparency of transaction fee you should paying to make a transfer occurs over the Ethereum blockchain https://ethgasstation.info/

Etherscan.io- allows you to verify and track transactions that are done across the Ethereum network https://etherscan.io/

Ethplorer.io- complete address analytics from historical balances to the volumes of trading, operations and transfers per token per day https://ethplorer.io/

Fungible token – is interchangeable token. An analogy with fiat currency is you can change a dollar to other dollar bill, it will be the same and have the same value.

F.U.D – Fear, Uncertainty and Doubt

FOMO- Fear of Missing Out

Fintech- Financial Technology. Companies produce solution that evolves around technology that aim to solve issues such as payment, investment, asset management etc

Fiat- a currency that the government declares as legal tender. USD, GBP, EUR

Fork- sometimes “hardfork” or “softfork” means splitting a source code to different development directions.

Full node- A node is any computer that connects to a network.

Gas limit- refers to the maximum amount of gas you're willing to spend on a particular transaction.

Genesis Block- The very first blockchain in a blockchain

Gwei- The denomination of an Ether, used to calculate Gas to push a transaction forward. 1 ETH = 1000000000 Gwei

Hashing- Hashing is a method of cryptography that converts any form of data into a unique string of text.

HODL- Hold on to dear life

Hype- intensive marketing and publicity that exaggerates the benefits of a product/service

Hyperledger- is an open source collaborative effort created to advance cross-industry blockchain technologies. It is a global collaboration, hosted by The Linux Foundation, including leaders in finance, banking, Internet of Things, supply chains, manufacturing and Technology.

Initial coin offering- also known as ICO is a fund-raising vehicle used by a blockchain venture/start-up. Cryptographic tokens will be issued to contributors of a venture.

Initial Public offering- also known as IPO or stock market launch is a type of public offering in which shares of a company are sold to institutional investors and usually also retail (individual) investors.

KYC- Also known as Know Your Customer is the process of a business verifying the identity of its clients. The objectives of KYC guidelines are to prevent tokens from being used, intentionally or unintentionally, by criminal elements for money laundering 

IoT: Abbreviation of Internet of things is the network of physical devices, vehicles, home appliances, and other items embedded with electronics, software, sensors, actuators, and connectivity which enables these things to connect, collect and exchange data, creating opportunities for more direct integration of the physical world 

Market order- is a buy or sell order to be executed immediately at current market prices. As long as there are willing sellers and buyers, market orders are filled.

Market maker- A market maker is a person or brokerage house that is always prepared to buy and sell tokens in order to provide liquidity to the markets.

Margin Trade- margin trading refers to the process whereby individual investors buy more stocks than they can afford to. Lending the funds from the exchange and then trading of the growth margin of digital asset.

Mainnet- carries out the functionality of transferring of cryptocurrency from sender to recipient. It is the actual end product of a network. (see also “Testnet”)

Mining- is the process by which transactions are verified and added to the blockchain very common in the Ether Network

Moon- A slang for a bullish movement of a token, as though it is heading to the moon

MultiSig- provides an additional layer of security, requires more than one signature to gain access

Peer to Peer- also known as P2P refers to direct dealing between Person A to Person B without the need or involvement of a middlemen exchange

Pump and Dump- pumping the value of a stock/token by spreading fake news and fake demand. Usually a group of investors target small cap token by buying large amount of holding creating a false demand. Eventually dumping it all for a profit.

Regulation- the action or process of regulating or being regulated

Security Token- are defined as any blockchain-based representation of value that is subject to regulation under security laws

Short- selling tokens that are borrowed from broker or lender, betting a stock/token will drop in value.

Smart Contract- are contracts whose terms are recorded in a computer language instead of legal language. Smart contracts can be automatically executed by a computing system, such as a suitable distributed ledger system.

Silicon Valley- is in the southern part of the San Francisco Bay Area in Northern California in the United States. The region is home to many of the world's largest technology companies including Apple, Cisco, Google, HP, Intel and Oracle.

Testnet- allow developer to test the transferring of a token without the use of an actual token (see also “Mainnet”)

Utility tokens- the unique characteristic of a Utility tokens is it is not designed as an investment. It is also known as user’s token which allows accessibility to products/services offered by an organisation
